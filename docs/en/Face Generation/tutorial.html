Guide to Using the Face Generation Feature

The Face Generation feature in AiCasso is incredibly versatile! It allows you to create stunning images by incorporating a specific face into various characters or styles. Whether you're imagining a knight, samurai, or any other character, AiCasso uses the input face to generate an image with that face integrated into the requested design.

Optional Parameters

If you want to add a bit more personality or control to your generated images, here are some options you can tweak:

  • Negative Prompt: This is where you can tell AiCasso what you don’t want to see in the image. If you leave this blank, AiCasso will just focus on the positive prompt.
  • Number of Denoising Steps: This controls how smooth and refined the image looks. You can choose between 21, 31, or 41 steps, with 41 being the default. More steps mean a cleaner, more polished image.
  • Guidance Scale: This helps the image stay true to your description. The scale ranges from 1 to 20, with 10 being the default. Higher numbers mean AiCasso will stick closer to your prompt.
  • S Scale: This is all about the structure of the face. It’s a bit technical, but essentially it adjusts the weight of the face structure. The default is 1.0, and you can go anywhere from 0.0 to 2.0.
  • Style: Want to give your image a specific vibe? You can choose a style like comic, chibi, 3D, anime, or realistic. Realistic is the default, but feel free to get creative!

And remember, AiCasso is pretty forgiving. You don't have to be a grammar wizard or get every detail perfect. Just describe what you want in your own words, and AiCasso will take care of the rest. Enjoy creating!
