Guide to Using the Image to Video Feature

The Image to Video feature in AiCasso lets you turn a single image into a dynamic video. Just describe what you want to see in your video, and AiCasso will make it happen!

Optional Parameters

If you want to get more specific with your video, here are some settings you can adjust:

  • Negative Prompt: Describe what you *don’t* want in the video. If left empty, AiCasso will just focus on the positive prompt.
  • Number of Frames: Choose how many frames (individual images) your video will have. You can select up to 25 frames, with 16 being the default.
  • Denoising Steps: This controls how smooth your video looks. More steps mean a cleaner video. You can set up to 50 steps, with 20 as the default.
  • Guidance Scale: This helps the video stick closely to your description. The range is from 1.0 to 8.0, and you can adjust the start and end values to guide how the video evolves.
  • Motion: Adjust how much movement is in your video. A higher value means more action. The range is from 0 to 127, with 60 as the default.
  • Noise Augmentation Strength: Add some noise to your video to give it a more textured look. The range is from 0 to 1, with 0 being the default.
  • Frames per Second (FPS): Set how fast your video plays. The default is 16 FPS, but you can go up to 16 for a faster playback.
  • Interpolation Method: Choose how the frames are blended together. The default is "lerp" (linear interpolation), but you can also use "slerp" (spherical linear interpolation) for different effects.
  • Clip Skip: Skip certain layers for a different video style. You can skip up to 2 layers, or leave it null for the default behavior.

Remember, AiCasso is pretty smart! You don’t have to get every detail perfect or use correct grammar. Just describe what you want, and AiCasso will do the rest. Have fun creating your videos!
